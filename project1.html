Covid News

date:06/07/2021

Britain’s daily coronavirus cases could reach 100,000 this summer as the government moves to end nearly all covid-related restrictions later this month, the country’s health secretary said Tuesday. Sajid Javid told BBC Radio that coronavirus case numbers, currently hovering around 25,000 each day, were expected to double ahead of the July 19 deadline to lift restrictions, as the more transmissible delta variant drives a new surge in infections. “As we ease and go into the summer, we expect them to rise significantly, and they could go as high as 100,000 case numbers,” Javid said. Even at its worst peak in January, Britain’s daily cases never surpassed 60,000 a day.

Kimmich injury concern

Joshua Kimmich's attempt to stop Erling Haaland yesterday ended in injury for the Germany midfielder. Initial reports suggest Kimmich has not suffered a cruciate ligament tear, but German sports magazine kicker are reporting that the 25-year-old has suffered a meniscus tear in his right knee, an injury that might require surgery and would see Kimmich likely out for the rest of the year. There is no official word yet from Bayern Munich yet, although confirmation of Kimmich's diagnosis and treatment is expected soon.
